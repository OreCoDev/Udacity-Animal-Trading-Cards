Puffin

Although similar in appearance, Puffins and penguins are not related. Unlike penguins, puffins are actually able to fly!

  • Scientific Name: Fratercula arctica
  • Average Lifespan: 30+ years
  • Average Weight:50grams
  • Habitat: Oceans

The Atlantic Puffin is often called teh clown of the sea. In flight, puffins flap their wings frantically to stay airborne, but underwater their wings become powerful flippers that allow the birds to catch small fish one by one until their beaks are full.
Source: All About Birds

Domestic Dog

Dogs were domesticated between 18,000 and 32,000 years ago

  • Scientific Name: Canis familiaris
  • Average Lifespan: 10-13 years; varies by breed with smaller dogs having longer lifespans
  • Average Weight:3 to 250 pounds; varies greatly by breed
  • Habitat: Habitats of dogs also vary greatly by breed. Some are comfortable in desert, some in the snow. Some are only happy curled up on the couch!

One-third of all households worldwide are estimated to have at least one dog. Although dogs are domesticated as pets, many breeds are capable of surviving in the wild. Dogs are the most popular pet in the world, with over one million dogs in the world.
Source: National Geographic

Domestic Cat

Cats were domesticated around 7500 BC; It was long believed that cats were domesticated in ancient Egypt

  • Scientific Name: Felis catus
  • Average Lifespan: 12-18 years
  • Average Weight:7.9-9.9 pounds
  • Habitat: Cats are everywhere!

The housecat is a domesticated mammal belonging to the family Felidae. It is a small, typically furry, carnivorous mammal with a loose, round body, and retractile claws.
Source: National Geographic Britannica
